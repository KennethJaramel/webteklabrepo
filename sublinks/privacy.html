The right to privacy states that every individual has the right to keep information about themselves to be hidden from public.

However, this right, like plenty of the others, is not absolute. During times of emergency or trouble, where it is vital for the government to know what is going on with the citizens of its country, certain information must be gathered such as those private to each individual.

Government institutions such as the National Statistics Office, NSA, and many others may keep said vital information about the individuals, however, it is important for them to keep these information private for they could be abused and used for the wrong purpose.

By ensuring this, it will allow individuals to live without worry and be more at ease.

For all human beings we have rights to have a privacy between our friends and family. And we have all the rights to have a protection of the law if we are in bad situation.

According to Article 17:

  • No one have the right to do bad interference between his/her privacy, home, family or correspondence, nor to the unlawful attacks on his/her honour and reputation.
  • No one should interfere the privacy of each family.
  • No one have rights to go to one house if there is no permission of the home owner.
